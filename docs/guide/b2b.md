## Overview

This comprehensive guide covers how to set up a Business-to-Business (B2B) company in Portugal,
specifically designed for professionals who want to work as contractors or freelancers while
maintaining a formal business structure. Setting up a B2B company in Portugal allows you to:

- Work as an independent contractor with Portuguese companies
- Benefit from potentially lower tax rates compared to employment
- Have greater flexibility in your professional relationships
- Access EU market opportunities
- Maintain professional autonomy while complying with Portuguese law

## Legal Business Structures

### 1. Unipessoal por Quotas (Unipersonal Limited Company)

**Best for**: Most freelancers and contractors

- **Minimum capital**: €1
- **Liability**: Limited to company assets
- **Tax rate**: 21% on first €25,000, then progressive rates
- **Social Security**: Mandatory contributions based on income

### 2. Sociedade por Quotas (Private Limited Company)

**Best for**: Partnerships or larger operations

- **Minimum capital**: €2 (distributed among partners)
- **Partners**: 2 or more
- **Liability**: Limited to company assets
- **Management**: More flexible structure

### 3. Estabelecimento Individual de Responsabilidade Limitada (EIRL)

**Best for**: Individual entrepreneurs wanting limited liability

- **Minimum capital**: €5,000
- **Liability**: Limited to assigned assets
- **Less common**: More complex structure

### 4. Empresário em Nome Individual (Sole Proprietorship)

**Best for**: Small-scale operations (not recommended for B2B)

- **No minimum capital**
- **Unlimited liability**: Personal assets at risk
- **Higher tax rates**: Progressive income tax

## Registration Process

### Step 1: Choose Your Company Name

1. **Check name availability** at the National Registry of Commercial Names (RNPC)
2. **Reserve your chosen name** (valid for 3 months)
3. **Consider trademark implications** for your business

### Step 2: Obtain Required Documentation

**Portuguese Tax Number (NIF)**

- Apply at Finanças (Tax Authority)
- Required for all business activities
- Can be obtained online or in-person

**Social Security Number (NISS)**

- Register at Segurança Social
- Required for social contributions
- Automatically issued for Portuguese citizens

### Step 3: Company Registration

**Online Registration (most efficient)**

1. Visit [eportugal.gov.pt](https://eportugal.gov.pt)
2. Use "Empresa na Hora" (Company in One Hour) service
3. Complete online form with:
   - Company details
   - Articles of association
   - Share capital information
   - Director/administrator details

**Physical Registration**

1. Visit a "Loja do Cidadão" (Citizen Shop)
2. Use "Empresa na Hora" service
3. Complete registration in approximately 1 hour
4. Receive immediate certification

### Step 4: Open Business Bank Account

**Required documents**:

- Commercial registry certificate
- Company bylaws/articles of association
- Tax identification number
- Personal identification
- Proof of address

## Tax Obligations

### Corporate Income Tax (IRC)

**Tax Rates (2024)**:

- **21%** on taxable income up to €25,000
- **25%** on taxable income above €25,000
- **Additional 3%** on income above €1.5 million
- **Additional 5%** on income above €7.5 million

**Tax Benefits**:

- **50% reduction** for first two years (qualifying companies)
- **Deductions** for business expenses
- **Double taxation agreements** with many countries

### Value Added Tax (IVA)

**Registration Requirements**:

- Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds €12,500
- Voluntary registration below this threshold
- Different rates: 6% (reduced), 13% (intermediate), 23% (standard)

**Digital Services**:

- Special rules for digital services to EU customers
- OSS (One-Stop-Shop) scheme available

### Social Security Contributions

**Mandatory Contributions**:

- **Workers**: 11% of gross salary
- **Company**: 23.75% of gross salary
- **Minimum base**: 1.5 × minimum wage (for directors)
- **Maximum base**: 12 × minimum wage

### Personal Income Tax (IRS)

**For Company Directors**:

- Subject to personal income tax on director fees
- Progressive rates from 14.5% to 48%
- Additional solidarity tax on high incomes

## Invoicing and Documentation

### Invoice Requirements

**Mandatory Information**:

- Sequential invoice number
- Issue date
- Company details (both parties)
- Tax identification numbers
- Description of goods/services
- Unit prices and quantities
- Tax rates and amounts
- Total amount due
- Payment terms

### Digital Invoicing

**AT Communication**:

- All invoices must be communicated to Tax Authority
- Use certified invoicing software
- Real-time reporting (SAF-T PT)
- QR codes on invoices

**Recommended Software**:

- InvoiceXpress
- Sage
- PHC
- Primavera
- FreshBooks

### Record Keeping

**Mandatory Records**:

- **General ledger**: All business transactions
- **Invoice register**: All issued and received invoices
- **Asset register**: Company assets and depreciation
- **Bank statements**: All business bank accounts
- **Tax documents**: All tax-related correspondence

**Retention Period**: 10 years for most business documents

## Legal Considerations

### Employment Law Compliance

**B2B vs Employment**:

- Clear contractual distinctions required
- Avoid "false contractors" classification
- Regular review of working relationships
- Document independence and autonomy

**Contract Elements**:

- Scope of work definition
- Payment terms and schedules
- Intellectual property rights
- Termination clauses
- Liability limitations

### Data Protection (GDPR)

**Compliance Requirements**:

- Data processing agreements
- Privacy policies
- Consent mechanisms
- Data subject rights procedures
- Breach notification protocols

### Intellectual Property

**Protection Strategies**:

- Trademark registration (INPI)
- Copyright documentation
- Trade secret policies
- Non-disclosure agreements
- Work-for-hire clauses

## Ongoing Compliance

### Monthly Obligations

1. **IVA Declaration** (by 20th of following month)
2. **Withholding tax payments**
3. **Social Security contributions**
4. **Monthly accounting updates**

### Quarterly Obligations

1. **IRC advance payments**
2. **Quarterly financial statements**

### Annual Obligations

1. **Annual tax return (Modelo 22)** by May 31st
2. **Annual accounts filing** at Commercial Registry
3. **Social Security annual declaration**

### Professional Support

**When to Hire Professionals**:

- **Accountant (TOC)**: Mandatory for most companies
- **Lawyer**: For complex contracts and compliance
- **Tax advisor**: For optimization strategies
- **Business consultant**: For growth and development
